# Võro verb conjugation: rules reference

Scope: verb conjugation, covering moods, tenses, person/number, voice, negation,
and the infinite (non-finite) forms. Noun declension is a separate resource.

Source: rules distilled and re-expressed from Sulev Iva, *Võru kirjakeele
sõnamuutmissüsteem* (Tartu Ülikooli Kirjastus, 2007), ch. 5. Cite accordingly.

**Notation vs. current spelling.** Forms are given in Iva's *standard*
(*Võru–eesti sõnaraamat* 2002) notation, not in phonetic/phonological
transcription. The glottal stop `q` is shown consistently in every form where
it can occur; overlength is marked with a leading `` ` ``. This is **not** the
simplified **2005 practical spelling**. Within the verb system the 2005 norm
writes `q` only in: the **infinitive** (*tetäq*), the **imperative 2sg**
(*tiiq*), the **indicative 3pl present and past** (*saavaq*, *kirotasõq*,
*saiq*, *kirodiq*), and **pre-negation present/past** forms (*ei tiiq*,
*es võtaq*). It is normally **not** written in the jussive, the imperative
plural, the conditional, or other forms, so the morphological *võtkuq*
(jussive) and *võtkõq* (imperative pl) are written *võtku* / *võtkõ* in current
practice, whereas *võtaq* (inf/2sg) and *võtvaq* (ind 3pl) keep their q.

## Notation

Cover symbols stand for a vowel that alternates by **front/back vowel harmony**,
chosen by the stem (identical to the noun reference):

| symbol | back stem | front stem |
| ------ | --------- | ---------- |
| `A`    | a         | ä          |
| `E`    | õ         | e          |
| `U`    | u         | ü          |
| `O`    | o         | ö          |
| `V`    | the appropriate harmonic/plural vowel |

Other conventions:

- `q` = glottal stop. It is **morphologically loaded on verbs**: it ends the
  imperative (`võtaq`), the infinitive (`võttaq`), the jussive (`võtkuq`), etc.,
  and is the visible exponent of several endings. But note that morphological
  presence ≠ written under 2005: the q is kept in writing only in the
  infinitive, imperative 2sg, indicative 3pl (present/past) and pre-negated
  present/past forms (see the header and 2005 note); elsewhere (jussive,
  imperative plural, conditional) it is normally dropped in current spelling.
- A leading `` ` `` marks third quantity (overlong grade); not written in normal
  orthography.
- `( )` = an optional element or an alternant.
- Palatalization is written with a trailing apostrophe / acute (`võt'`, `kõnõľ`);
  see the 2005 note below for when it is written at all.

## Orthography conventions (2005 reform)

The current written norm follows the corrections adopted by the Võru Institute's
dictionary working group on 12 September and 7 November 2005:

- **Glottal stop `q`** and **palatalization `´`** are written sparingly, only
  where needed to keep forms distinct, above all where they **distinguish
  meaning**. On verbs the 2005 norm keeps `q` in writing in exactly four
  environments: the **infinitive** (*tetäq*, *küssüq*), the **imperative 2sg**
  (*tiiq*, *kirodaq*), the **indicative 3pl present and past** (*saavaq*,
  *kirotasõq*; *saiq*, *kirodiq*), and the **present/past pre-negation** form
  after the negation word (*ei tiiq*, *es võtaq*). The jussive (`-guq/-kuq`) and
  the imperative plural (`-gEq/-kEq`) morphologically end in `q`, but under 2005
  this `q` is **normally not written**. Palatalization is written mainly as the
  imperfect marker (*võtç*, *masś*) and before a back vowel (*maŕaq*, *palïo*).
- **High õ (`y`)** is written as ordinary **õ**; it never distinguishes meaning.

> Source (Võru Institute): *"Võru keele uue kirjaviisi parandused on läbi arutatud
> ja vastu võetud Võru Instituudi sõnaraamatu töörühma koosolekuil 12. septembril
> ja 7. novembril 2005. Paranduste põhisisu on, et kõrisulghäälikut (q) ja
> peenendust (´) soovitatakse märkida senisest vähem, ainult kõige hädatarvilisemal
> puhul, eelkõige seal, kus need eristavad tähendust. Kõrget õ-häälikut (y)
> märgitakse edaspidi tavalise õ-ga, sest see häälik ei erista kunagi tähendust."*

## Core principles

1. **Finite vs infinite forms.** Verbs split into finite (person-marked) forms
   (indicative, conditional, quotative, imperative, jussive, plus the
   passive/impersonal) and infinite forms (supine, infinitive, gerundive and
   participles). The full inventory is 13 infinite and 44 synthetic finite forms.

2. **Present indicative person-number endings** (added to the present stem):

   | person | ending |
   | ------ | ------ |
   | sg 1   | `-Ø` (no ending; historical *-n* lost) |
   | sg 2   | `-t` |
   | sg 3   | `-Ø` **or** `-s` (two conjugation types, see #4) |
   | pl 1   | `-miq` |
   | pl 2   | `-tiq` |
   | pl 3   | `-vAq` **or** `-sEq` |

   The imperfect has its own past formatives and person/number morphology; in
   particular, pl 3 is normally the past form plus final `q`, not present-tense
   `-vAq`/`-sEq` (e.g. *võtiq*).

3. **Pronoun-conditioned plural endings.** In pl 1 and pl 2 the ending may be
   omitted when a personal pronoun precedes; the preferred literary pattern is
   pronoun + short form, or no pronoun + full ending: *võtamiq* but *mi võta*;
   *võtatiq* but *ti võtat*. With no pronoun, the ending is expected.

4. **Two third-person conjugations.** A defining South-Estonian trait: the present
   3rd person comes in an **s-final** type (*eläs*, *kasus*) and an **endingless**
   type (*võtt*, *jää*), with the plural 3rd patterning as `-sEq` vs `-vAq`
   accordingly. Treat this as a lexical property of each verb (it is encoded in the
   classification table).

5. **Two negation strategies**, a distinctive feature. Võro negates a verb in
   **two** standard ways:
   - **Pre-negation** (*eeseitus*): a particle **before** the verb, namely `ei`
     (present and perfect), `es` (past and pluperfect), `ärq`/`ärkeq` in the
     prohibitive imperative, and `ärkuq` in the jussive. The verb itself often
     keeps `-q`: *ei võtaq*, *es võtaq*.
   - **Post-negation** (*tagaeitus*): an **enclitic after** the verb, namely
     `-Vi(q)` (present), `-Vs` (past), realized by vowel copy: *võta-aiq*,
     *võta-as*, *olõ-õi*, *olõ-õs*.

   Both negate the same clause, but they are **not** merely interchangeable
   stylistic variants. Pre-negation (with *ei/es*) is general across the whole
   South-Estonian area; post-negation (the enclitic) is restricted. Within
   South Estonian it is known **only in Võro and Seto**, and within Võro it is
   most at home in East/Central Võro, rarer in the west. In the quotative it is
   very rare; the indicative present/perfect, quotative and (partly) conditional
   take *ei(q)*, while the indicative simple past/pluperfect and (partly)
   conditional take *es*.

## Moods and tenses (markers + *võtma* 'take')

**Indicative present**: bare person endings (see #2). sg3 `-Ø`/`-s`,
pl3 `-vAq`/`-sEq`.
*võta, võtat, võtt; võtamiq, võtatiq, `võtvaq.*

**Indicative imperfect (simple past)**: a past formative
`-i / -si / -ssi / -ś / -E / -iE / -sE / -Ø` followed by the relevant
person/number morphology; sg3 takes `-i´` (palatalizing), `-ś`, `-E`, `-iE`,
`-sE` or `-Ø`.
*võti, võtit, võt'; võtimiq, võtitiq, võtiq.*

**Perfect / pluperfect** are analytic active forms: the auxiliary **olõma** ('be')
in the present (perfect) or imperfect (pluperfect) + the **past active
participle** `-nUq`. Passive/impersonal perfect and pluperfect forms use the
corresponding passive participial forms (`-t/-d` in the singular, `-tUq/-dUq`
in the plural).
*Perfect:* olõ/olõt/om/olõmiq/olõtiq/ommaq + `võtnuq.
*Pluperfect:* olli/ollit/oll'/ollimiq/ollitiq/olliq + `võtnuq.

**Conditional** (*tingiv*): the same form is used in all persons (sg and pl).
Two present-conditional formations coexist: the older, fullest South-Estonian
synthetic marker `-siq` ~ `-ssiq` (*võtasiq*, *käkissiq*; the s-geminate depends
on quantity/syllable count), and a newer **`nU(q)`-conditional** shaped like the
past participle (*jäänüq*, *kirotanuq*), originally West-Võro but now widespread
in the east and Seto too. The past conditional is analytic: *olõma* in the
conditional + the `-nUq` participle (*olõssiq `võtnuq*). Negation: `nUq`-forms
take *es* (*es `andnuq*), while the synthetic `-siq` forms show both (*ei
annasiq* ~ *annasi eiq*, and past-side *es*). Under 2005 the final `q` of the
conditional is normally not written.

**Quotative / reported** (*kaudne*): `-vAt`, `-v`, `-bEv`.
*võttõv ~ `võtvat* (pr); past *ollõv / olõvat `võtnuq*.

**Imperative** (*käskiv*): essentially a **second-person** category in
productive use. sg2 `-q` (*võtaq*); the plural ending is `-gEq`/`-kEq`
(*`võtkõq*), which serves both pl2 and (formally identically) pl1. A pl1
imperative is not a separate productive form. (sg1, sg3, pl3 have no
imperative.) Prohibitive: **ärq** (sg2) / **`ärkeq** (pl2) + the jussive-style
form: *ärq `võtkuq*, *`ärkeq `võtkõq*. The plural 1st person has its own
prohibitive *ärmiq* (= *ärq* + pl ending) + verb: *ärmiq annamiq* 'let's not
give'.

**Jussive** (*möönev*): `-guq`, `-kuq`. *`võtkuq.*

**Potential** (*võimalikkuse*): `-nEs` (sg3), `-nEsEq` (pl3). Recorded only
from the oldest 19th-century informants and in folk-song texts; **not used in
modern Võro literary or common language** (treat as obsolete, not merely
unproductive).

**Passive / impersonal**: voice markers `-dA / -tA / -vvA / -iA`; present 3rd
`-tAs/-dAs` (*`võetas*, pl `võetasõq), past 3rd `-ti/-di` (*`võeti*, pl `võediq);
past participle sg `-t/-d` (*võet*), pl `-tUq/-dUq` (*`võeduq*).
Modern-usage note: unlike Estonian, Võro historically had a *personal* passive
inflecting in all persons, but that full paradigm is **archaic**. In modern
Võro (literary and dialectal) the present and imperfect mainly use the
**impersonal** (3rd-person) forms above; generalized sg3 forms function as
impersonal. The forms here are given for systemic completeness.

## Combined verb classification (Table 21)

Each combined type (**KT**) is the intersection of three sub-classifications,
encoded in the **FTA** code as `F.T.A`:

- **F**: formative distribution;
- **T**: stem-final alternation pattern;
- **A**: grade-alternation pattern.

**VES** is the *Võru–eesti sõnaraamat* type number; **tüüpsõnad** is the model
verb. The diagnostic columns are: infinitive, past participle (active and
passive), and indicative present sg 1 (*maq* 'I') / sg 3 (*tä* 's/he') and
indicative imperfect sg 1 / sg 3, the four finite cells that fix the conjugation.
A leading `` ` `` marks overlong grade (not written normally); `( )` marks
alternants or optional segments.
