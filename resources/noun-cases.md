# Võro noun declension: rules reference

Scope: case and number formation for nouns, adjectives and numerals
(the main *noomen* word classes). Pronoun forms are mentioned where they behave
as special examples or exceptions. Verb conjugation is a separate resource.

Source: rules distilled and re-expressed from Sulev Iva, *Võru kirjakeele
sõnamuutmissüsteem* (Tartu Ülikooli Kirjastus, 2007), ch. 4. Cite accordingly.

**Notation vs. current spelling.** Forms are given in Iva's *standard*
(*Võru–eesti sõnaraamat* 2002) notation, not in phonetic/phonological
transcription. Iva keeps this fuller notation deliberately: the glottal stop
`q` is shown consistently in every formative where it can occur, with the
commonly-dropped variant in parentheses (e.g. `-niq (-ni)`), and overlength is
marked with a leading `` ` ``. This is **not** the same as the simplified
**2005 practical spelling**, which writes `q` only in a short list of
meaning-distinguishing environments (see the 2005 section below) and never
marks overlength. So a form like *taloniq* here is the morphological form;
in current practical writing it is normally *taloni*.

## Notation

Cover symbols stand for a vowel that alternates by **front/back vowel harmony**,
chosen by the stem:

| symbol | back stem | front stem |
| ------ | --------- | ---------- |
| `A`    | a         | ä          |
| `E`    | õ         | e          |
| `U`    | u         | ü          |
| `O`    | o         | ö          |
| `V`    | the stem's plural vowel: one of `i o E A` |

Other conventions:

- `q` = glottal stop (kõrisulghäälik). In the productive case endings shown
  below (terminative, abessive, comitative) it is historically present but
  **usually left unwritten** in current practical spelling. Both variants are
  listed as e.g. `-niq (-ni)`. It **is** written in several
  meaning-distinguishing environments, above all the **nominative plural**, and
  also in certain lexical q-final nouns (two-syllable e/õ- or u/ü-final words
  such as *kastõq*, *seemeq*, *oluq*, *lühüq*) and some adverbs/pronouns
  (*siiäq*, *sinnäq*, *ammuq*, *veidüq*, *esiq*). See the 2005 section.
- `( )` around a final element marks an optional / commonly-dropped component.
- Standard orthography does **not** mark overlength (third quantity). A few forms
  are therefore **homographs distinguished only by quantity**, not spelling. See
  the partitive/illative note below.

## Orthography conventions (2005 reform)

The current written norm follows the corrections adopted by the Võru Institute's
dictionary working group on 12 September and 7 November 2005. Operative rules for
generation:

- **Glottal stop `q`** and **palatalization `´`** are written sparingly, only
  when needed to keep forms distinct, and above all where they **distinguish
  meaning**. Do not mark them by default.
- **High õ (`y`)** is written as ordinary **õ**. It never distinguishes meaning
  and has no separate letter in the standard orthography.

Per Iva's appendix 3, within the noun system the 2005 norm keeps `q` in writing
mainly in these environments: (a) the **nominative plural** (*puuq*, *mõtsaq*);
(b) certain **two-syllable e/õ- or u/ü-final nouns** (*kastõq*, *imeq*,
*seemeq*, *pehmeq*, *oluq*, *lühüq*); and (c) some **adverbs and pronouns**
(*siiäq*, *sinnäq*, *eeläq*, *ammuq*, *veidüq*, *kinniq*, *esiq*).

Consequences for the tables below: the `q` shown in the terminative, abessive and
comitative endings is normally **omitted** in writing. The **nominative-plural
`q` is kept**, because there it is meaning-distinguishing. Palatalization marks on
citation forms are likewise reduced to the meaning-distinguishing cases. The
leading `` ` `` (overlength) is never written in either norm.

## Core principles

1. **The genitive singular is the base.** Almost every concrete case in the
   singular is built by adding its ending to the genitive-singular stem, which is
   always vowel-final (g *oppaja* → all *oppajalõ*, in *oppajan*). Exceptions:
   the endingless short illative and the `tE/dE`-illative attach to a different
   stem (see illative).

2. **Plural concrete cases do not have one universal base.** In many vowel-plural
   and contaminated types they are built on the partitive-plural stem, typically
   in weak grade, by inserting a plural formative before the case ending
   (e.g. ad *jalol*, el *kullõst*). However, `tE/dE` plural types use the
   genitive-plural stem for plural concrete cases, the plural illative has its
   own strong-grade/additive behavior, and the plural comitative is built on the
   genitive-plural stem and is strong-grade or contaminated (see plural sections).

3. **Vowel harmony** governs every ending containing a cover symbol: a back stem
   takes the back realization, a front stem the front one (all *talolõ* vs
   *keelele*; ab *taloldaq* vs *keeleldäq). The **comitative `-gaq` is the sole
   exception**: it is invariably back, with no front `*-gäq`.

4. **Stem-final grade alternation** affects a set of word types where the
   genitive is weak but the other cases are strong (g *perädü* → ad *perätül*,
   ill *kotussõhe*). In these types the genitive and the comitative stay weak;
   everything else uses the strong stem.

5. **Locative cases are very often replaced by an analytic postposition phrase**
   (genitive + postposition). The common pairs are listed under each case.

## Singular case endings

### Nominative (n): no ending
No marker. The word ends in a vowel or a consonant; consonant-final stems arise
from final-vowel loss, and the stem vowel then surfaces in the genitive. Some
nouns are q-final or h-final (*läteq* 'spring', *herneq*/*herneh* 'pea',
*asõq* 'bed'); h survives mainly in eastern Võro and is otherwise replaced by q.
Historical i-stem palatalization of the nominative singular is **no longer
written** in current orthography.

### Genitive (g): no ending
Always vowel-final. This is the base stem for the concrete cases. A subset of
word types has a weak-grade genitive (g *kotusõ*, *hõragu*) while the other cases
are strong (ill *kotussõhe*, el *hõrakust*).

### Partitive (p): `-t` / `-d` / `-Ø` / (`-tt`, `-dA`)
- `-t` is the default and attaches to vowels or consonants: *oppajat*,
  *inemist*, *saabast*.
- `-d` appears only after a long vowel, diphthong or voiced consonant:
  *kuud*, *üüd*, *tütärd*, *kannõld*.
- **Endingless `-Ø`** is very common; the form then stands in strong or
  *lengthened* grade. In two-syllable first-quantity stems this lengthening is
  the only thing distinguishing the partitive: *pinni*, *kalla*, *tallo*,
  *häpü* (contrast Estonian, where nominative = genitive = partitive *kala*).
- `-tt` occurs in a few special nominal forms, e.g. *kätt*, *vett*.
  `-dA` occurs in pronouns such as *kedä*, *midä*, *tedä*
  (with eastern geminate variants *ketä*, *mitä*).

### Illative (ill): many endings, or `-Ø`
The most type- and dialect-variable case. Endings: `-he` (commonest; suffixal,
harmony-immune), `-tE`, `-dE`, `-htE`, `-hE`, `-hA`, `-hU`, `-ho`, or **`-Ø`**.
- `-he` attaches to stems whose genitive is two-syllable third-quantity or
  longer: *lättehe*, *kotussõhe*, *parandajahe*.
- `-tE` attaches to s/t consonant stems and `nE`/`Us` words: *inemiste*,
  *ehitüste*, *kätte* (type *käsi* takes `-ttE`).
- `-dE` after voiced consonants and some long-vowel stems: *kiilde*, *verde*,
  *kuudõ*.
- **Endingless / short illative** (the "additive") stands in strong or **overlong**
  grade. Two sub-cases must be kept apart:
  - In two-syllable weakening-grade types and the *kanarik*, *vari*, *asi* types,
    partitive and short illative **fully coincide**, both in spelling *and*
    quantity: p = ill *`jalga*, *`haina*, *`silmä*, *`kuuli*, *`asja*.
  - In two-syllable first-quantity **geminate** types, the only contrast is
    quantity: partitive *tarrõ*, *tallo*, *häpü* (2nd quantity) vs illative
    *`tarrõ*, *`tallo*, *häppü* (3rd quantity). Here quantity alone separates them.
    In normal orthography (which does not mark overlength) the two are homographs.

### Inessive (in): `-n`
Attaches to the genitive stem: *talon*, *keelen*, *saapan*. Literary/standard
Võro generally prefers `-n`. Regional variants `-h` and `-hn` also exist
(eastern; Rõuge/Haanja).
Postpositions: *seen ~ sisen* (esp. plural): *haino seen*.

### Elative (el): `-st`
*talost*, *keelest*, *saapast*. Postpositions: *seest ~ sisest*: *vii seest*.

### Allative (all): `-lE`
*talolõ*, *keelele*, *saapalõ*. A short (2nd-quantity) geminate `ll` survives
after a stressed short vowel: *mullõ*, *sullõ*, *tälle*. Postpositions:
*pääle, kätte, mano* (western Võro *pääleq, manoq*): *lavva pääle*, *esä kätte*.

### Adessive (ad): `-l`
*talol*, *keelel*, *saapal*. Postpositions: *pääl, käen, man*: *lavva pääl*,
*esä käen*.

### Ablative (abl): `-lt`
*talolt*, *keelelt*, *saapalt*. Postpositions: *päält, käest, mant*:
*lavva päält*, *esä käest*.

### Translative (tr): `-s`
Half-long s, **written single**: *talos*, *keeles*, *saapas*. (Eastern Räpina/Seto
and Karula have a `-st` translative: *suurõst*, *mehest*.) Postposition: *jaos*:
*tuu jaos*.

### Terminative (ter): `-niq` (`-ni`)
*taloniq*, *keeleniq*, *saapaniq*. The final q is usually unwritten.

### Abessive (ab): `-ldAq` (`-ldA`)
*taloldaq*, *keeleldäq*, *saapaldaq*. This `ldAq` is the Võro-specific standard
ending; northern/western dialects use `-ttAq`/`-dAq` (more Tartu-like). Final q
usually unwritten.

### Comitative (kom): `-gaq` (`-ga`)
Attaches to the **genitive** stem: *talogaq*, *keelegaq*, *saapagaq*. **Always
back-vocalic**, with no harmony and no `*-gäq`. Final q usually unwritten. A geminate
`-kaq` is, in almost all Võro and in the literary standard, limited to personal
pronouns: *mukkaq, sukkaq, miikaq, tiikaq* (from *muq + gaq*). Dialectally
(North-Võro and Tartu-type) it can also appear on other words: *võtmidõkka*,
*puiõkka*, *massinidõkkaq*.

## Plural

### Number markers
- **Nominative plural: `-q`** added to the genitive-singular stem:
  g *mõtsa* → pl n *mõtsaq*. In connected speech the q may assimilate to the next
  word's initial consonant or drop, but `-q` is the standard marker.
- **Genitive plural** is formed with one of:
  - a **vowel marker** `i / o / E` (genitive and partitive plural then coincide;
    the vowel depends on the stem vowel): *pinne*, *tallõ*, *tarri*, *jalgo*.
    Older East-Võro/Seto also has an **`A`-plural** in some types (*talla*,
    *häpä*, *tükkä* for standard *tallõ*, *häpe*, *tükke*), and western Võro an
    `u`-plural (*kannu*, *naklu*) for standard `o`;
  - a **`tE`/`dE` marker**: *hanśadõ*, *neiodõ*, *lätete*;
  - a **contaminated marker** `idE / iE(ijE) / isi / idsi`: *oppajidõ*,
    *lättide*, *inemiisi*, *ehitüisi* (with `idsi` on adjectives, `isi` on nouns).
- **Partitive plural** uses `-it (-id)`, `-sit` (type 1 and diphthong stems),
  a vowel marker, or a contaminated `isi/idsi/iE` marker. A short `i` normally
  *replaces* the stem vowel: *hobõsõ + i + t → hobõsit*.

### Plural concrete-case formatives
For many types, plural concrete cases are formed from the partitive-plural stem,
usually in weak grade, by adding a plural formative plus the singular case
ending. The base is nevertheless type-dependent: `tE/dE` plural types use the
genitive-plural stem, and the illative and comitative have special behavior.
The main formative shapes are a vowel-multiple `-V…`, `-dE…`, `-tE…`, and
contaminated `-isi…` / `-idsi…`:

| case | plural formatives (ending shown on first) |
| ---- | ----------------------------------------- |
| in   | `-Vn` (-in/-on/-En/-An), `-dEn`, `-tEn`, `-isin`, `-idsin` |
| el   | `-Vst`, `-dEst`, `-tEst`, `-isist`, `-idsist` |
| all  | `-VlE`, `-dElE`, `-tElE`, `-isilE`, `-idsilE` |
| ad   | `-Vl`,  `-dEl`,  `-tEl`,  `-isil`,  `-idsil` |
| abl  | `-Vlt`, `-dElt`, `-tElt`, `-isilt`, `-idsilt` |
| tr   | `-Vs`,  `-dEs`,  `-tEs`,  `-isis`,  `-idsis` |
| ter  | `-Vniq`,`-dEniq`,`-tEniq`,`-isiniq`,`-idsiniq` |
| ab   | `-VldAq`,`-dEldAq`,`-tEldAq`,`-isildAq`,`-idsildAq` |
| ill  | mostly `-he` or endingless, in **strong** grade (e.g. *tarri(he)*) |

Example (back stem *jalg* / front stem *keel*): ad *jalol*, all *jalolõ*,
in *hanśadõn*, el *võrokõisist*.

### Plural comitative
The **plural comitative** is *not* built on the partitive-plural stem.
It uses the **genitive-plural** stem and is strong-grade or contaminated:
pl g *lehti*, *jalgo*, *rebäside* → pl kom *lehtigaq*, *jalgogaq*, *rebäsidegaq*.
Formatives: `-Vgaq`, `-dEgaq`, `-tEgaq`, `-isigaq`, `-idsigaq`.

## Agreement (ühildumine)

A modifier (adjective, etc.) **agrees** with its head noun in:

- all six **locative** cases, and the **translative**: *suurõn mehen*,
  *hääs oppajas*, *korgist mäkist*, *piinüis palos*;
- the **terminative** in older Võro (*poolõniq seereniq*), though modern usage
  increasingly leaves the modifier in the genitive (*poolõ seereniq*).

A modifier **stays in the genitive** (does not agree) in the **abessive** and
**comitative**: *hää oppajaldaq*, *suuri mehildäq*, *hää oppajagaq*,
*piinüide pallogaq*.

## Unproductive cases

- **Essive (es)**: has merged formally with the inessive (`-n`, regionally
  `-h`/`-hn`): *noorõh*, *väikun*. The Estonian-style `-nA` essive (*arstina*) is
  a recent Estonian-influenced import, not traditional Võro. Traditional Võro uses
  the inessive, the nominative/translative, or a *ku*-phrase instead.
- **Instructive (ins)**: no ending, plural only. Coincides with the weak-grade
  vowel-plural stem: *ütsi*, *käsi*, *jalo*, *keeli*. Borderline between case and
  adverb.
- **Prolative (pro)**, ending `-ldE`: *maildõ*, *merilde*. Productive for new words:
  *telefonildõ*, *e-postildõ*, *internetilde*. Often interchangeable with
  instructive forms (*tükä ~ tükelde*).

## Combined noun classification (Table 9)

Each combined type (**KT**) is the intersection of three sub-classifications,
encoded in the **FTA** code as `F.T.A`:

- **F**: formative distribution (which plural and case formatives the word takes);
- **T**: stem-final alternation pattern;
- **A**: grade-alternation pattern.

**VES** is the corresponding type number in the *Võru–eesti sõnaraamat* (several
dictionary types may map to one combined type); **tüüpsõna** is the model word.
The remaining columns give the diagnostic forms: singular genitive, singular
partitive, singular illative, and the plural genitive / partitive / illative /
allative.

Reading the table: a leading `` ` `` marks third quantity (overlong grade) and is
not written in normal orthography; forms in `( )` are alternants; `(s)`, `(t)`,
`(r)` etc. mark a consonant that may or may not surface. Where one KT lists
several model words, they share the FTA pattern but differ in dictionary
classification.
