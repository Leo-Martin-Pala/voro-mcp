# Võro orthography and standard language: rules reference

Scope: the **writing system** (*kiräviis*) and **standard-language** orientation —
the alphabet, the glottal stop `q`, palatalization, the high õ, length spelling
(`s`, `h`, `j`, overlength), negation spelling, and guidance on producing the
Võro Institute literary standard rather than a narrow dialect. It closes with a
short anti-Estonian-leakage vocabulary aid.

This file deliberately does **not** repeat morphology. Case and number formation
live in `noun-cases.md`; conjugation, the two third-person types and the two
negation systems live in `verb-conjugation.md`. Those files carry their own
domain-specific summary of where `q` is written; this file is the general
orthography reference they point back to.

Sources: rules distilled and re-expressed from the Võro-language Wikipedia
articles *Võro kiil*, *Võro kiräkiil*, *Võro kiräviis* and *Kakkõhelü
ettetulõmisõ tävvelik nimekiri* (fiu-vro.wikipedia.org, CC BY-SA), together with
the Võru Institute's 2005 spelling corrections. Cite accordingly.

## Notation

Same conventions as the morphology files:

- `q` = glottal stop (*kakkõhelü* / kõrisulghäälik).
- `` ` `` (leading) = third quantity (overlength); **never written** in normal
  orthography.
- `( )` around `q` = the glottal stop is **optional / normally left unwritten**
  in that position.
- **Bold** example sets mark positions where `q` is meaning-distinguishing and
  therefore **kept in writing**.
- `´` = palatalization mark; in practice often replaced by a plain apostrophe `'`.
- Front/back vowel-harmony cover symbols (`A E U O`) are explained in
  `noun-cases.md` and reused here unchanged.

## The alphabet (võro tähistü)

The Võro–eesti sõnaraamat gives the standard inventory:

```
Aa Bb (Cc) Dd Ee Ff Gg Hh Ii Jj Kk Ll Mm Nn Oo Pp Qq Rr Ss (Šš)
Tt Uu Vv (Ww) Õõ Ää Öö Üü (Xx) Yy (Zz) (Žž) ´
```

- Letters in `( )` are **foreign letters**, used only for foreign words and for
  names in foreign or old-style spelling. `c` also appears somewhat in poetry and
  belles-lettres; `f` only in loanwords.
- **`q` and `y` are not foreign letters in Võro.** `q` marks the glottal stop
  (*maq, kaeq, kalaq*). `y` historically marked the high, i-like õ (*sys, nyna,
  myyk*); since the 2005 corrections this sound is written with ordinary **õ**,
  so `y` is effectively retired from running text (see "High õ" below).
- Palatalization is carried by the mark `´` over or after a consonant, giving
  `ś ń ľ ť ḱ h́` etc. With several palatalized consonants in a row, only the
  **last** carries the mark: *pall´o*, *arst´*, *pand´*.

Letters are pronounced largely as in Estonian; `q` and `y` are the main
exceptions above. Most consonants (except `j` and `q`) have hard and palatalized
variants.

## The 2005 corrections (headline principles)

The current norm follows the corrections adopted by the Võru Institute's
dictionary working group on 12 September and 7 November 2005:

- Mark the glottal stop (`q`) and palatalization (`'`) **less** than older usage
  did — only where genuinely necessary, **above all where they distinguish
  meaning**. Do not mark them by default.
- Write the **high õ** (formerly `y`) with ordinary **õ**; it never distinguishes
  meaning.

> Source (Võru Institute): *"Võru keele uue kirjaviisi parandused on läbi arutatud
> ja vastu võetud Võru Instituudi sõnaraamatu töörühma koosolekuil 12. septembril
> ja 7. novembril 2005. Paranduste põhisisu on, et kõrisulghäälikut (q) ja
> peenendust (') soovitatakse märkida senisest vähem, ainult kõige hädatarvilisemal
> puhul, eelkõige seal, kus need eristavad tähendust. Kõrget õ-häälikut (y)
> märgitakse edaspidi tavalise õ-ga, sest see häälik ei erista kunagi tähendust."*

## Glottal stop (kakkõhelü, q)

`q` is a full phoneme in Võro and frequently distinguishes word forms
(e.g. singular *maa* vs plural *maaq*; imperative *tulõ* vs *tulõq*). The 2005
norm splits its many occurrence positions into two groups.

### Where q IS written (meaning-distinguishing)

Write `q` in these environments:

- **a) Nominative plural:** **puuq, mõtsaq, suurõq majaq, vahtsõq saapaq,
  vereväq vabarnaq.**
- **b) Indicative present and past 3rd person plural:** **saavaq, kirotasõq,
  võtvaq, ommaq; saiq, kirodiq, võtiq, olliq.**
- **c) The -q infinitive (II infinitive):** **kaiaq, tetäq, ollaq, tiiniq,
  võttaq, herädäq, kirotaq, häbendelläq.**
- **d) Imperative 2nd person singular:** **kaeq, küsüq, võtaq, puhkaq, kirodaq.**
- **e) Present/past pre-negation (edeeitüs), after the negation word:**
  **ei tiiq, ei käseq, ei võtaq, es kirodaq, es häbendelleq;** impersonal
  **ei tetäq, ei võetaq, ei kästäq, es kirotõdaq, es häbendeldäq.**
- **f) Two-syllable nouns ending in e/õ or u/ü:** **kastõq, paisõq, lõunõq
  (lõunaq); imeq, hoonõq; seemeq, asõq; pehmeq, kahrõq; oluq, ohuq, lühüq.**
- **g) Core adverbs and pronouns:** **siiäq, sinnäq, eeläq, toonaq, ammuq,
  veidüq, kinniq; esiq.**

(For where this `q` falls inside the noun and verb paradigms specifically, see
the per-domain summaries in `noun-cases.md` and `verb-conjugation.md`.)

### Where q is optional / normally NOT written

`q` occurs in many further positions, but there it is **normally left
unwritten**; `(q)` below marks the optional glottal stop. It may still be written
occasionally for emphasis, to set a word off, or for rhyme.

q as a further plural marker:

- past participle plural: (ommaq) mõstu(q), (ommaq) eledü(q), (olliq) söödedü(q),
  (mar'aq) kor'adu(q); (ommaq) mõsknu(q), (ommaq) elänü(q);
- plural 1st/2nd person present and past: teemi(q), näemi(q), võtami(q),
  kirotami(q); tiiti(q), näeti(q), võtati(q), kirotati(q); teimi(q), näimi(q),
  võtimi(q), kirotimi(q); teiti(q), näiti(q), võtiti(q), kirotiti(q);
- impersonal simple-past plural: as'aq kaotõdi(q), piniq malgutõdi(q),
  viläq määtedi(q).

Other optional-q endings, clitics and forms:

- post-negation (tagaeitüs): tii ei(q), käse-i(q), võta-i(q), nätä-i(q),
  küsü-i(q), usu-i(q), häö-i(q), mõsõ-i(q);
- nuq/nüq past participle: (om) tennü(q), (om) mõsknu(q), (om) elänü(q);
- abessive: saamalda(q), võtmalda(q), kargamalda(q), kirotamalda(q);
- terminative: seeni(q), kooni(q), põh'ani(q), rõnnoni(q), hambini(q),
  hummoguni(q);
- comitative: käega(q), mukka(q), pujaga(q), tütrega(q), oppajaga(q),
  kadajaga(q);
- imperative 3rd / imperative-plural and their negation: kaegõ(q), küsüge(q),
  võtkõ(q), kirotagõ(q); ärq kaegu(q), ärq küsügu(q), kaegu-i(q), võtku-i(q);
- jussive: kaegu(q), küsügu(q), võtku(q), kirotagu(q);
- present singular 2nd person: (sa) tiide(q), näede(q), võtadõ(q), kirotadõ(q),
  häbendellede(q);
- the gi/ki clitics: kiäki(nä)(q), midägi(nä)(q), tetäki(nä)(q),
  söömäldagi(nä)(q);
- conditional: teesi(q), kaesi(q), kargasi(q), vähändäsi(q); tennü(q), kaenu(q),
  karanu(q), vähändänü(q);
- further adverbs/pronouns and particles: paokilla(q), kükäkillä(q), parhilla(q),
  mano(q), ala(q), taha(q), maha(q), õigõ(q); ärä(q), är(q); ma(q), sa(q),
  mi(iq), ti(iq), nä(äq); a(q), aga(q), ega(q), ku(q), nigu(q), jo(q), no(q),
  jälle(q), kül(q), tere(q), ja(q).

## Palatalization (peethelle pehmehüs, ´/')

The palatalization mark goes over or after the consonant; the practical
apostrophe `'` is widely used in its place (especially online and in the Võro
Wikipedia, e.g. *kor'as'* for *koŕaś*).

### Write palatalization when it is

- a) the **past-tense marker**: võtt', pand', käänd', murd', masś, laul', pill',
  oll', tull', karaś, tapõl', kergüt';
- b) **before a back vowel**: mar'aq, var'oq, lag'a, lakk'a, pall'o, kah'o,
  tśura, tśauk, ńaugma.

### Do NOT normally write palatalization when it is

- a) at the **end of a noun**: pall, kuul, kiil, üts, kats, pang, pätt, täht,
  kruun, kumm, kapp, parv, kurk, kõik, vask, suur, käng, sälg; hopõn, repän,
  jummal, pakan, kavval, tütär;
- b) at the **end of a verb**, when not the past-tense marker: ots, vaht, sund,
  mähk, tsurk, kamm;
- c) **before a consonant**: sitkaq, mustkaq, vask; otsma, vahtma, arstma,
  pakma, pruuvma, kamma;
- d) **before `i` and `j`**: veli, padi, kosima, kassiq, tsiga, kallis; patja,
  asjo, kandja, võtja;
- e) **before front vowels**: lävi, nägo, veleq, keele, küle, näläne, käng, sälg.

It **may** be written at noun endings etc. when special attention is wanted —
e.g. to keep meanings apart (*palk* vs *palk´*, *vaśk* vs *vask´*), for rhyme, or
similar emphasis.

(Note: the source standard lists *käng, sälg* under both "noun-final" and "before
front vowels"; left as in the source.)

## High õ (korgõ õ)

Write the high õ with **ordinary õ**: *mõõk, mõõtma, nõna, sõna, mõni, sõs, õks,
sõsar, kõik, sõa*. Before 2005 it was written `y` (*myyk, sys, nyna*). Only in
dictionary headwords and where strictly necessary is a raising mark used: an
upward arrow `ˆ` under the õ.

## Length and a few letter-level spelling rules

Overlength (third quantity) is otherwise not marked, but several letters have
single/double conventions:

- **Raised mid-high overlong vowels** are written with the same letters as the
  high vowels — `ii`, `uu`, `üü`: *kiil, keele, kiilt; kuul, kooli, kuuli;
  küük, köögi, küüki*. Overlong õ is written plain: *mõõk, mõõga, mõõka*.
- **`s`** is single when short (*viis, kuus, kaas, raas*) or half-long (*katus,
  parandas, suurõs, tetäs, makas, miis, kaes*), and double when long (*mass,
  päss, huss, häüss, poiss, kauss*).
- **`h`** is single when short (*raha, ilosahe*) or half-long (*tah, mõtsah*),
  and double when long (*rahha, kihhä*) — **but** long `h` is written single
  after a long vowel or diphthong (*maaha, tüühü, vaihõl, laiha*) and in the
  third syllable of four-syllable words (*rabahama, unõhuma, saisahutma,
  kabõhõnõ, sagõhõlõ*).
- **`j`** word-finally occurs only after `i` (*tekij, näkij*); otherwise a final
  is `i` (*vitäi, petäi, jallai*). Word-internally after `i`, `j` is unwritten
  (*vaia, saia, kaiaq, saiõ, kaiõ*) except in the agent-noun suffix *-ja/-jä*
  (*nägijät, tegijil*). Short `j` is single; long/overlong `j` is double (or
  written `i`): *maja*, partitive *majja* (or *maia*), illative *majja*.

## Negation spelling

The two negation **systems** (pre-negation *ei/es* and the post-negation
enclitic) are described in `verb-conjugation.md`. Orthographically:

- **Post-negation** is written with a hyphen plus the negation ending, or (less
  often) the full negation word: *olõ-(õ)iq*, *olõ-(õ)s*, *nätä-(ä)iq*,
  *nätä-(ä)s*, or *olõ eiq*, *olõ es*.
- **Pre-negation** keeps `q` on the verb where the meaning-distinguishing rule
  applies: *ei võtaq*, *es võtaq* (see the q list above, group e).

## Marking spectrum and the diacritic problem

Different outlets sit at different points on the marking spectrum. The Võru
Institute standard marks `q`, palatalization and (where needed) the high õ; the
newspaper *Uma Leht* marks palatalization but not `q` or the high õ; some
materials (e.g. the *Uma Pido* song festival) mark `q` with an apostrophe.

A practical caution from the *Võro kiräviis* article: the apostrophe `'` is also
used for the glottal stop, for elision and for quotation marks, and these,
together with palatalization `´`, look nearly identical and are easily confused.
The same sentence under three marking styles:

```
Lugõja kai lehe üle. Imä pand' mar'a pangi.        (minimal q)
Lugõja' kai' lehe' üle. Imä pand' mar'a' pangi.    (apostrophe for q)
Lugõjaq kaiq leheq üle. Imä pand' mar'aq pangi.    (full q)
```

For a consistent output, pick the Võru Institute standard (`q` written in the
meaning-distinguishing positions, palatalization with `´`/`'`) and apply it
throughout a whole text.

## Standard-language orientation

Aim for the **Võro Institute literary standard** (*standardkiil*) — the variety
used in Institute teaching materials, dictionaries and most published Võro — not
a single village's dialect. The standard deliberately balances a maximally old,
"concentrated" Võro against a South-Estonian average, steering clear of the most
sharply divergent local features so the text reads naturally across all corners
of Võromaa.

Concrete standard choices (full detail in the morphology files):

- Keep **second-syllable `o`**, not `u`: *talo*, *elo*, *kodo* (not *talu*,
  *elu*, *kodu*).
- The **inessive** is written `-(h)n`, read as `-n`, `-h` or `-hn`, with the
  `-n` variant generally preferred in the standard: *mõtsa(h)n*, *talon*.
- **Vowel harmony is alive** in Võro (unlike modern Estonian): endings take a
  front or back form by the stem — *külä*, *küsünüq*, *hõbõhõnõ*; *talolõ* vs
  *keelele*. The cover-symbol notation for this is in `noun-cases.md`.
- Võro has **two present 3rd-person types** (s-final *kirotas* vs endingless
  *and*) and **two negation strategies** — see `verb-conjugation.md`.

When coining or borrowing, the standard prefers native derivation and careful
adaptation, and avoids loans where a Võro word already exists. A few Institute
coinages: *alossäädüs* 'principle', *pääjuht* 'director', *teedüstü* 'file',
*hüvvütämä* 'to enrich', and new senses for old words (*helü* also 'speech
sound', *kelmeq* also '(plastic) film').

## Distinctive vocabulary (vs Estonian)

Common everyday words where Võro differs markedly from Estonian (Estonian →
Võro). Many Võro forms are closer to Finnish than to Estonian.

| Estonian | Võro |
| -------- | ---- |
| punane   | verrev |
| soe      | lämmi |
| jahe     | oigõ |
| õde      | sõsar |
| uus      | vahtsõnõ |
| koer     | pini |
| pöial    | päss |
| pesema   | mõskma |
| tänavu   | timahavva |
| hunt     | susi |
| mäger    | kähr |
| laupäev  | puulpäiv |
| surema   | kuulma |
| sõstar   | hõrak |
| kask     | kõiv |
| nutma    | ikma |
| märkama  | rõbahtama |

Function words and pronouns:

- Personal pronouns: *ma(q) ~ mina*, *sa(q) ~ sina*, *timä ~ tä(ä)*; plural
  *mi(iq)*, *ti(iq)*, *nimäq ~ nä(äq)*.
- Question words: *miä, kiä, kuis, ku pall'o, kon, kohe, kuna, määne, mis, mille,
  minktarbis*.
- Numerals 1–10: *üts(ś), kats(ś), kolm, nelli, viis(ś), kuus(ś), säidse,
  katõssa, ütessä, kümme*; 11 *ütśtõist*, 20 *katśkümmend*, 100 *sada*,
  1000 *tuhat*.
- Negation patterns (vs Estonian): *saq ei anna* ~ *saq anna-aiq* (Est. *sa ei
  anna*); *maq tulõ-õiq* (Est. *ma ei tule*); past *saq anna-as*, *maq tulõ-õs*.
